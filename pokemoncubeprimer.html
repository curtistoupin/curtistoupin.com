Power

Badges

The aim for this cube is to be slightly (but only slightly) less powerful than the MTGO Vintage Cube. Just like the vintage cube, there are a lot of extremely high power cards. Firstly, there is 16 badges (those from the Kanto and Johto games). While most of these badges are roughly the power level of signets, in that they cost 2 mana and can produce mana of a color pair, 6 are closer to powered down moxes. Be on the lookout for the Zephyr Badge, Cascade Badge, Plain Badge, Volcano Badge, Earth Badge, and Mineral Badge.

Balls

The various balls act as additional sources of fast mana. The three best of these being the PokeBall, a functionl reprint of Sol Ring, the Master Ball, a functional reprint of Black Lotus, and the GS Ball, which is a powered up mox (although there is only one!).

Blue Spells

Just like in the real Vintage Cube, there are some busted blue spells. Three of the biggest of this category are Meditate, a powered down Ancestral Recall, Hypnosis, a powered down Time Walk, and Wild Missingno Was Caught, a (mostly) functional reprint of Timetwister.

Starter Pokemon

It wouldn't be Pokemon if you couldn't choose your favourite starter. Starter is a new mechanic like companion that lets you cast a spell from your sideboard but only if it's the first spell you cast in a game. Every color has a Starter, plus a Starter that can slot into any color deck. Be on the look out for Bulbasaur, Charmander, Squirtle, Pikachu, Meowth, and Eevee.

Buildarounds and Archetypes

White Aggro

White has a lot of tools to disrupt opponents while applying a lot of pressure. Look out for cards like Jigglypuff which does double duty protecting your own creatures while helping to push damage through, especially after evolving into Wigglytuff. Pair it with cards like Pidgey and Pidgeot to pressure your opponent's life total to close out the game.

Blue Good Stuff

Blue is just generically good. It does a little bit of everything. It has counterspells, planeswalkers, disruption, card draw, and so on. It also complements white for flickering and artifact synergies, red for card draw synergies, black for reanimation synergies, and green for combo synergies, and more. We'll go over those a little later. For now here's a preview of some quality blue cards.

Black Life Loss

Black has a minor subtheme of paying or losing life. On the face of it, this sounds like a drawback, but there are a few cards that reward you heftily for losing life, and some cards that enable these by letting you pay or lose large amounts of life at a time.

Red Deck Wins

Always a classic, and it returns with some new hits. Draft aggressive creatures to get in early damage and burn spells to deal the last few points of damage for the win.

Green Ramp

Make a lot of mana and cast things ahead of curve. It's something Grass pokemon like Bellsprout and Oddish excel at.

UW Artifacts

Blue and white combine with their artifact synergies. This can take the form of a fair strategy with synergies or cheating expensive artifacts into play.

UW Flicker

With good etb effects and ways to flicker your creatures, blue and white also combine to get a lot of value out of flickering its stuff.

UB Mill Control

With a combination of strong removal, counterspells, and strong threats, blue and black form a potent control deck with a minor mill subtheme as a way of closing out games without ever attacking.

UB Reanimator

Blue is great at drawing and discarding cards, and black is great at returning creatures from the graveyard to the battlefield. these combine into a great way to cheat huge creatures onto the battlefield.

BR Spectacle Aggro

Black and red love putting on a show. It can come out fast and hit hard, and sometimes even be rewarded for its effort by casting cheaper spells.

BR Midrange

When in doubt, Jund 'em out. Some of these cards pull double duty and can be used with less of an aggressive focus and more of a value midrange focus as well.

RG Lands

Many of the red and green cards center around playing lands, or lands entering the graveyard for value.

RG Land Destruction

As good as red-green is at playing lands, it's just as good at destroying them.

GW Hatebears

Green and white are both good at stopping the things your opponent is doing. Back it up with a lot of power and toughness to close the game before your opponent can get things off the ground.

WB Death and Taxes

White and black is the color of ghosts. These cards combine in ways to make life difficult for your opponent, whether by making their spells cost more or preventing them from attacking.

WB Midrange Reanimator

With the help of the Ghost trainer Agatha, white and black become a potent midrange reanimator deck, able to fight a fair game but with the power of large reanimated threats to back up the plan.

BG Rock

The classic black-green good stuff plan. Make them discard their best card then follow up with a threat, and beat them to death before they can rebuild. There is also a subtheme of venturing into the dungeon in this color pair, although the dungeons have been revamped for this cube.

BG Counters

Black-green is the color of poison, and like the poison pokemon they're based on, these cards win by weakening the opponent with -1/-1 counters and watching them slowly wither thanks to proliferating.

GU Big Stuff

GU is one of the best if not the best combations at cheating big stuff into play.

GU Glitch

There are some well known glitches in the early generations of Pokemon, and some of the best known of these have made it into the cube. From the game breaking item duplication glitch using missingno, to the hidden Glitch City, these are effects that are very powerful, possibly game winning on their own, but just like in-game, require a little bit of setting up to get the full effect by casting other glitch cards.

UR Tempo Control

Blue and red both have a minor subtheme of drawing extra cards. While drawing extra cards is always good in its own right, it never hurts to get a little extra effect out of it.

UR Cheatypants

Blue and red are also both great at cheating expensive creatures and artifacts into play. Pair these cards with something big to take control of the game way ahead of the curve.

RW Impulse

Red and white in this cube has a subtheme of being able to play cards exiled from the top of your library, as well as rewarding you for doing so.

RW Combo

RW is the pair with the most combos in this cube. Below are some of the cards to be on the watch for, but we'll cover the interactions in more detail in the combos section.

Grixis Wheels

Draw-sevens are of course good in plenty of decks, but they are at their most effective when you can make them one sided or punish your opponent for drawing extra cards.

Jeskai Combo

Some of the gold cards add on to the RW combo deck if you're able to play or splash blue.
