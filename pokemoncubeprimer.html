Power

Badges

The aim for this cube is to be slightly (but only slightly) less powerful than the MTGO Vintage Cube. Just like the vintage cube, there are a lot of extremely high power cards. Firstly, there is 16 badges (those from the Kanto and Johto games). While most of these badges are roughly the power level of signets, in that they cost 2 mana and can produce mana of a color pair, 6 are closer to powered down moxes. Be on the lookout for the Zephyr Badge, Cascade Badge, Plain Badge, Volcano Badge, Earth Badge, and Mineral Badge.

Balls

The various balls act as additional sources of fast mana. The three best of these being the PokeBall, a functionl reprint of Sol Ring, the Master Ball, a functional reprint of Black Lotus, and the GS Ball, which is a powered up mox (although there is only one!).

Blue Spells

Just like in the real Vintage Cube, there are some busted blue spells. Three of the biggest of this category are Meditate, a powered down Ancestral Recall, Hypnosis, a powered down Time Walk, and Wild Missingno Was Caught, a (mostly) functional reprint of Timetwister.

Starter Pokemon

It wouldn't be Pokemon if you couldn't choose your favourite starter. Starter is a new mechanic like companion that lets you cast a spell from your sideboard but only if it's the first spell you cast in a game. Every color has a Starter, plus a Starter that can slot into any color deck. Be on the look out for Bulbasaur, Charmander, Squirtle, Pikachu, Meowth, and Eevee.

Mana Fixing and Lands

Good decks require good mana fixing. Each color pair has four dual lands of those colors. Some of them are just function reprints while some of them are new for the cube, but they should all be helpful in building your mana base.

Fetchlands

No reason to mess with a good thing. The first cycle is a function reprint of the 10 existing fetch lands.

OG Duals

Again, if it ain't broke, don't fix it. The next 10 are just a functional reprint of the original dual lands. That is, they are fetchable with fetchlands and enter untapped.

Encounter Lands

Wild pokemon live all over the place. The next cycle of lands are also fetchable, and can enter untapped, but doing so will give your opponent a wild encounter. You're going to have to choose between having the land enter the battlefield tapped and dealing with your opponent's new Pokemon.

City Lands

Towns and cities are always hustling and bustling. You have to choose whether to carry on with your Pokemon adventure or to get side tracked by the latest goings on in the story. You have to make a similar choice with the city lands. They are a cross beween tapped and untapped lands. You can choose to use the mana this turn if you need to play on curve, but that's going to mean you have to lose out on it next turn. But giving you the option makes room for flexibility and interesting decisions to be made.

Buildarounds and Archetypes

White Aggro

White has a lot of tools to disrupt opponents while applying a lot of pressure. Look out for cards like Jigglypuff which does double duty protecting your own creatures while helping to push damage through, especially after evolving into Wigglytuff. Pair it with cards like Pidgey and Pidgeot to pressure your opponent's life total to close out the game.

Blue Good Stuff

Blue is just generically good. It does a little bit of everything. It has counterspells, planeswalkers, disruption, card draw, and so on. It also complements white for flickering and artifact synergies, red for card draw synergies, black for reanimation synergies, and green for combo synergies, and more. We'll go over those a little later. For now here's a preview of some quality blue cards.

Black Life Loss

Black has a minor subtheme of paying or losing life. On the face of it, this sounds like a drawback, but there are a few cards that reward you heftily for losing life, and some cards that enable these by letting you pay or lose large amounts of life at a time.

Red Deck Wins

Always a classic, and it returns with some new hits. Draft aggressive creatures to get in early damage and burn spells to deal the last few points of damage for the win.

Green Ramp

Make a lot of mana and cast things ahead of curve. It's something Grass pokemon like Bellsprout and Oddish excel at. Draft mana dorks to make a lot of mana, as well as giant green or colorless things like Exeggcutor and Nidoking.

UW Artifacts

Blue and white combine with their artifact synergies. This can take the form of a fair strategy with synergies or cheating expensive artifacts into play.

UW Flicker

With good etb effects and ways to flicker your creatures, blue and white also combine to get a lot of value out of flickering its stuff.

UB Mill Control

With a combination of strong removal, counterspells, and strong threats, blue and black form a potent control deck with a minor mill subtheme as a way of closing out games without ever attacking.

UB Reanimator

Blue is great at drawing and discarding cards, and black is great at returning creatures from the graveyard to the battlefield. these combine into a great way to cheat huge creatures onto the battlefield.

BR Spectacle Aggro

Black and red love putting on a show. It can come out fast and hit hard, and sometimes even be rewarded for its effort by casting cheaper spells.

BR Midrange

When in doubt, Jund 'em out. Some of these cards pull double duty and can be used with less of an aggressive focus and more of a value midrange focus as well.

RG Lands

Many of the red and green cards center around playing lands, or lands entering the graveyard for value.

RG Land Destruction

As good as red-green is at playing lands, it's just as good at destroying them.

GW Hatebears

Green and white are both good at stopping the things your opponent is doing. Back it up with a lot of power and toughness to close the game before your opponent can get things off the ground.

WB Death and Taxes

White and black is the color of ghosts. These cards combine in ways to make life difficult for your opponent, whether by making their spells cost more or preventing them from attacking.

WB Midrange Reanimator

With the help of the Ghost trainer Agatha, white and black become a potent midrange reanimator deck, able to fight a fair game but with the power of large reanimated threats to back up the plan.

BG Rock

The classic black-green good stuff plan. Make them discard their best card then follow up with a threat, and beat them to death before they can rebuild. There is also a subtheme of venturing into the dungeon in this color pair, although the dungeons have been revamped for this cube.

BG Counters

Black-green is the color of poison, and like the poison pokemon they're based on, these cards win by weakening the opponent with -1/-1 counters and watching them slowly wither thanks to proliferating.

GU Big Stuff

GU is one of the best if not the best combations at cheating big stuff into play.

GU Glitch

There are some well known glitches in the early generations of Pokemon, and some of the best known of these have made it into the cube. From the game breaking item duplication glitch using missingno, to the hidden Glitch City, these are effects that are very powerful, possibly game winning on their own, but just like in-game, require a little bit of setting up to get the full effect by casting other glitch cards.

UR Tempo Control

Blue and red both have a minor subtheme of drawing extra cards. While drawing extra cards is always good in its own right, it never hurts to get a little extra effect out of it.

UR Cheatypants

Blue and red are also both great at cheating expensive creatures and artifacts into play. Pair these cards with something big to take control of the game way ahead of the curve.

RW Impulse

Red and white in this cube has a subtheme of being able to play cards exiled from the top of your library, as well as rewarding you for doing so.

RW Combo

RW is the pair with the most combos in this cube. Below are some of the cards to be on the watch for, but we'll cover the interactions in more detail in the combos section.

Grixis Wheels

Draw-sevens are of course good in plenty of decks, but they are at their most effective when you can make them one sided or punish your opponent for drawing extra cards.

Jeskai Combo

Some of the gold cards add on to the RW combo deck if you're able to play or splash blue.

5 Color

It can be a little tough to make the manabase work for 5 colors, but if you can pull it off the payoffs can be worth it. In addition to having access to whatever the best card that comes your way is, there are some very powerful cards that only someone playing all 5 colors can take advantage of.

Combos

Since it can be a bit daunting to look at a cube and try and see the combos between the cards. This is a list of all the combos I'm aware of.

Infinite Life

Play Ditto when you have a Chansey in play. Copy Chansey, then flicker Chansey. When Chansey comes back in, flicker Ditto. Repeat this loop, gaining 2 life each time until you break the cycle.

Play Chansey while you have an Articuno or vice versa. Continually use their abilities to flicker each other, just like the combo with Ditto.

Play Nurse Joy when you have a Chansey in play. Use Nurse Joy's -2 ability to flicker Chansey. When Chansey enters, use its ability to flicker Nurse Joy, and repeat, gaining infinite life.

Play Double Team, a functional reprint of Splinter Twin, targeting your Chansey. Tap Chansey to create a copy of it, and use the copy's ability to flicker Double Team. When it re-enters, put it on the copy. Every time a new one enters, the other Chanseys will gain you life. Repeat until you've gained enough life. Since all the Chanseys are all tapped, you can't attack, but hopefully the infinite life will be able to get you to the finish line.

Play Ditto with a Nurse Joy out, and use the Ditto to copy Nurse Joy. The new Nurse Joy will be an artifact, so it can be copied with Smeargle. Use the new Nurse Joy to flicker Smeargle. Since an artifact entered this turn, it will have haste, and can copy Nurse Joy again. Repeat until you've gained enough life.

Twin Style Combos

Double Team is a functional reprint of Splinter Twin. So be on the lookout for Pestermite style effects. Of course, Double Team combos with Scyther, one of its most famous users. Put Double Team on Scyther, create a copy of it which will untap the original Scyther. Repeat as many times as you like and then attack for lethal.

Chansey is a combo machine. It's a little more fragile, but Chansey and Double Team can be an instant kill as well. The technique is the same, but you wait until your opponent's end step to do it. You'll gain the infinite life as well, but you'll untap on your turn with infinite Chansey tokens which can kill your opponent.

Magnemite also works with Double Team and Smeargle in much the same way. When it enters, you use its ability targeting itself, which untaps it and gives it haste. When you make a copy of it with Double Team, use the copy's ability to untap the original, and repeat. Then attack with all the tokens you've made.

If you have a Ditto and a Smeargle, you can use them to copy each other on your opponent's end step, then untap and attack with as many tokens as you want.

With a Chansey on the battlefield, use Team Rocket Jesse and James' -2 ablity to create a copy of it. Use Chansey's ability to flicker Jesse and James, and use the new Jesse and James to create another Chansey. Repeat ad infinitum, then attack.

With an Articuno on the battlefield, use Team Rocket Jesse and James' -2 ablity to create a copy of it. Use Articuno's ability to flicker Jesse and James, and use the new Jesse and James to create another Articuno. Repeat ad infinitum, then attack.

With a Ditto in the graveyard (or in hand if you want to cast it), use Team Rocket Jesse and James to make a copy of your Raichu. When Raichu enters, return Ditto to the battlefield and use it to copy Jesse and James, and sacrifice the original to the legend rule. Use the copy Jesse and James to make another copy of Raichu. When the new Raichu comes in, use it to bring back the original Jesse and James. Repeat this way, alternating between sacrificing Ditto and the real Jesse and James. Then you have infinite hasty Raichus you can attack with for the win.

Infinite Mana

Call From the Skies lets you play cards from exile until end of turn. Food chain exiles a creature to produce one more mana than it costs to cast it. This means you can exile a creature to food chain, then recast it, netting one mana, and repeat as many time as you want. This gets you infinite mana in any color. Although your infinite mana can only be used to cast creature spells, it should be fairly easy to find a way to win from here. Probably the most straightforward choice is Forretress. You can cast it, remove all its counters to deal damage directly to your opponenent, then exile it to Food Chain and re-cast it. Repeat until your opponent is dead.

Free Spells

Silver Wing makes one spell a turn cheaper by one mana of each color. Rainbow Wing allows you to pay one mana of each color to cast spells. Put these two effects together and it makes one spell each turn free. You could take advantage of this to cast something huge like Dragonite for free. Or you could cast Gyarados without tapping any of your lands, meaning its effect when it enters the battlefield will only impact your opponent. As long as you're stocked up on big spells to freecast, it shouldn't be too hard to make this combo work for you.
